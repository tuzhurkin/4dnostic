who is recommended to undergo check up

Who cares about staying healthy

Who cares about staying healthy. Many diseases, even serious ones, can be asymptomatic for a long time, and then develop rapidly. In order not to waste time and eliminate the disease at an early stage, a complete diagnosis of the body is necessary.

Who has bad heredity

Who has bad heredity. You are at risk if you have relatives with oncological, cardiac or genetic diseases. Regular check-ups will help detect pathology in time.

Who has isolated complaints

Who cares about staying healthy. Many diseases, even serious ones, can be asymptomatic for a long time, and then develop rapidly. In order not to waste time and eliminate the disease at an early stage, a complete diagnosis of the body is necessary.

Who has bad habits and an unhealthy lifestyle.

Who has bad habits and an unhealthy lifestyle. This could be tobacco smoking, unbalanced diet, regular alcohol consumption, and inactive lifestyle. If any of these points are about you, it is better not to put off going through a check-up.

01.

Do I have to sign up for a check-up?

We don't recommend it because it's a waste of time and money. No reputable international medical organization welcomes it. The unnecessary tests are bound to find a lot of deviations from some reference standards, which in general do not affect the body in any way. But once detected, they will increase your anxiety, can make you resort to unnecessary medical treatment, and sometimes even lead to unnecessary surgery with possible complications. Why is this necessary?

We are supporters of evidence-based medicine and recommend only those tests and exams that are proven to be effective and can do more good than harm.
